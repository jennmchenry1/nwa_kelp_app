To get maps of the three projected community trajectories overlay 1) the complete kelp loss layer over and 2) classified the Bray Curtis Similarity layer for a given SSP scenario.

Bray-Curtis similarities < 0.25 we are classified in our analysis as "persistence zones" because they have more similar relative  likelihoods of occurrence of the kelp species to the present day.

Bray-Curtis similarities >= 0.25 are classified in our analysis as "turnover zones" because they more dissimilar to the present day in terms of the relative likelihoods of occurrence.

The complete kelp loss layer shows places where one or more kelp species is present in the present-day, but absent in a given SSP scenario. 

If you overlay all three, you can see the projected change in community trajectory by SSP.
